Premium Bhutan Treks

Embark on unforgettable Himalayan adventures through Bhutan's most spectacular landscapes

Snowman Trek

One of the world's most challenging and rewarding treks, the Snowman Trek takes you through remote Himalayan valleys and across high mountain passes. This epic journey offers unparalleled views of Bhutan's pristine wilderness.

Itinerary: 25 days covering Paro, Shana, Soi Thangthangkha, Jangothang, Lingshi, Chebisa, Shomuthang, Robluthang, Limithang, Laya, Rodophu, Narethang, Tarina, Woche, Lhedi, Thanza, Tshorim, Gangkar Puensum Base Camp, Geshe Woma, Warathang, Dur Tsachu, Tshochenchen, Duer Village, and finally Bumthang.

Best Time: Late September to October

25-30 Days 5,320m Max Altitude Extremely Challenging $7,599

Bumthang Cultural Trek

Explore the cultural heartland of Bhutan through this beautiful trek that combines natural beauty with rich cultural experiences. The Bumthang Valley is often called the "Switzerland of Bhutan" for its stunning landscapes.

Itinerary: 3 days covering Jakar, Ngang Lhakhang, Tahung, and Mesithang. Includes visits to Kurjey Lhakhang, Jambay Lhakhang, and the sacred Mebar Tsho (Burning Lake).

Best Time: March-May & September-November

3-4 Days 3,800m Max Altitude Easy to Moderate $1,299

Druk Path Trek

A beautiful and relatively short trek that connects Paro and Thimphu valleys. This route offers stunning views of Himalayan peaks, crystal clear lakes, and visits to ancient monasteries.

Itinerary: 6 days covering Paro, Jele Dzong, Jangchulakha, Jimilang Tsho, Simkotra Tsho, Phajoding, and Thimphu. Includes visits to Taktsang Monastery and Tashichho Dzong.

Best Time: March-June & September-November

5-6 Days 4,200m Max Altitude Moderate $1,899

Dagala Thousand Lakes Trek

This trek takes you through a spectacular region dotted with pristine alpine lakes. The route offers excellent trout fishing opportunities and breathtaking views of the Himalayan range.

Itinerary: 6 days covering Genekha, Gur, Labatama, Panka, and Chamgang. Includes panoramic views of Everest, Kanchenjunga, and Jomolhari, and opportunities for trout fishing in pristine lakes.

Best Time: April-June & September-October

5-6 Days 4,520m Max Altitude Moderate to Challenging $1,799

Laya Gasa Trek

One of Bhutan's most challenging and rewarding treks, this route takes you to the remote Layap villages and offers spectacular mountain scenery, hot springs, and unique cultural experiences.

Itinerary: 14 days covering Paro, Shana, Soi Thangthangkha, Jangothang, Lingshi, Chebisa, Shomuthang, Robluthang, Limithang, Laya, Koina, Gasa, and Punakha. Includes visits to remote Layap villages and relaxation in Gasa hot springs.

Best Time: March-May & September-November

12-14 Days 5,005m Max Altitude Challenging $4,299

Bhutan Cultural Tours

Discover the rich cultural heritage and breathtaking landmarks of the Himalayan kingdom

Taktsang Lhakhang (Tiger's Nest)

Perched on a cliffside 900 meters above the Paro Valley, this sacred monastery is Bhutan's most iconic landmark. The hike to Tiger's Nest is a spiritual journey through pine forests with breathtaking views.

Itinerary: Full day tour starting with a scenic drive to the base, followed by a 2-3 hour hike to the monastery. Includes guided tour of the monastery, lunch at the cafeteria with stunning views, and return hike.

Inclusions: Transportation, guide, entrance fees, and lunch

Paro Valley Full Day Tour Moderate Hike $199 per person

Phobjikha Valley

A beautiful glacial valley and winter home to the endangered black-necked cranes. This pristine ecosystem offers stunning landscapes, nature trails, and the Gangtey Monastery overlooking the valley.

Itinerary: 2-day tour including transfer from Thimphu, visit to Gangtey Monastery, nature walk through the valley, black-necked crane information center, and traditional farmhouse experience with local cuisine.

Inclusions: Transportation, guide, accommodation, meals, and entrance fees

Wangdue Phodrang 2 Days / 1 Night Wildlife Viewing $399 per person

Punakha Dzong

The magnificent fortress at the confluence of the Pho Chhu and Mo Chhu rivers. This architectural masterpiece served as Bhutan's capital until 1955 and remains an important religious and administrative center.

Itinerary: Full day tour from Thimphu including Dochula Pass, Chimi Lhakhang (Fertility Temple), Punakha Dzong, and suspension bridge walk. Optional visit to Khamsum Yulley Namgyal Chorten.

Inclusions: Transportation, guide, lunch, and entrance fees

Punakha Full Day Tour Cultural Heritage $199 per person

Dochula Pass

Located at 3,100 meters, this mountain pass offers spectacular 360-degree views of the Himalayan range. The site features 108 memorial chortens built in honor of Bhutanese soldiers.

Itinerary: Day trip from Thimphu including scenic drive to Dochula Pass, visit to Druk Wangyal Lhakhang, nature walk in Royal Botanical Park, and traditional Bhutanese lunch with mountain views.

Inclusions: Transportation, guide, lunch, and park entrance fees

Thimphu-Punakha Highway Full Day Tour Scenic Viewpoint $149 per person

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about traveling to Bhutan

What are the visa requirements for Bhutan?
All tourists (except Indian, Bangladeshi and Maldivian passport holders) need a visa to visit Bhutan. We handle the visa process as part of your tour package. You'll need to provide a clear copy of your passport and a passport-sized photo. The visa fee is included in your daily tariff.
What is the best time to visit Bhutan?
The best times are spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November). Spring offers beautiful rhododendron blooms and pleasant weather, while autumn has clear skies perfect for mountain views and festivals. However, Bhutan is beautiful year-round with each season offering unique experiences.
What should I pack for Bhutan?
Pack layers as temperatures can vary significantly. Include warm clothing, comfortable walking shoes, rain gear, sunscreen, and a hat. Don't forget your camera for the stunning landscapes! For trekking, we provide a detailed packing list based on your specific itinerary.
Is Bhutan expensive to visit?
Bhutan has a daily tariff system that covers accommodation, meals, transportation, guide services, and sustainable tourism fees. While this makes Bhutan more exclusive than some destinations, it ensures high-quality experiences and supports the country's sustainable development.
How difficult are the treks in Bhutan?
Bhutan offers treks for all fitness levels, from easy cultural walks to challenging expeditions like the Snowman Trek. We assess your fitness level and recommend appropriate treks. All treks include experienced guides, cooks, and porters to ensure your safety and comfort.
